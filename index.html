Know an Indian IT Scientist Born in March

🇮🇳 Prof. C. R. Rao (10 March 1920 – 22 August 2023)

Calyampudi Radhakrishna Rao was one of the greatest statisticians the world has ever known — and proudly, an Indian who shaped the mathematical foundations of modern information technology.

Born on 10 March 1920 in Hadagali, Karnataka, Prof. Rao's contributions include the Cramér–Rao Bound (fundamental to AI/ML), Rao–Blackwell Theorem (core to modern machine learning), and the Score Test. His work laid the groundwork for machine learning, neural networks, image processing, and information theory.

Over 75+ years, he authored 475+ papers and 14 books, received 38 honorary doctorates, the Padma Vibhushan, and the International Prize in Statistics (2023).

As we embark on our journey in IT, let us remember that the deepest innovations come from the strongest mathematical foundations.
